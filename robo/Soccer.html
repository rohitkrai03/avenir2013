Problem Statment

Design a manually controlled mechanical bot, which can push or kick a medium sized plastic ball. The prime motive of this game is to push or kick the ball in opponent's goalpost and avoid the ball from going into yours.
The bot with more goals(point) will win the game.

Rules:


General Rules:

  • > The maximum team size is 4.
  • > They must all be present students of an engineering institution. They may or may not be from the same institute.
  • > No participant can be a member of two teams. No two teams can share the same robot.
  • > The event co-ordinator is free to make any change to the above rules at any time. Any such change will be duly notified to the participants as and when required. The decision of the event coordinator will be final and binding.
  • > Participants must carry valid college id proof.

Technical details:

  • > Team members will not be allowed to touch any part of arena, only organizer are allowed to handle the arena in any situation, the team will be disqualified whose member found touching arena.
  • > You are not allowed to make any changes to your bot once the game has started.
  • > Participants are not allowed to keep anything inside arena other than the bot. power supply must be outside of the arena.
  • > The time measured by organizers will be final and used for scoring of the teams. Time measured by participants is not acceptable for scoring.
  • > Organizing team will not be responsible for any kind of damage to your bot.
  • > Any team which is not able to complete the task, whose scoring is done using judging criteria.
  • > Organizers decision will be final and binding in case of any dispute.
  • > All the game play related decisions will be made on spot by the referee for the game.
  • > In case of violations there can be penalties from penalty spot on referee discretion.
  • > If a bot becomes immobile it will be disqualified.
  • > Damaging the arena will lead to disqualification.

Game Overview:

  • > Game will be played with light plastic or polymer balls with size similar to golf ball.
  • > Bots can move in any part of the arena.
  • > In case the bot scores a goal or ball leaves the arena, ball will be awarded to opposing bot.
  • > The game will then restart from within starting range.
  • > Only first start will be from centre in which rear edge of both bots should touch the goal line, till the start of the game.
  • > Side will be decided by toss.in alternate way it will cahnge.
  • > Ball start will always be by a bump or kick not by moving or dragging the ball.
  • > Bot cannot keep the ball on halt for more than 10 seconds by any contact or shielding, in such a case foul will be awarded and the opponent will start the game from his starting zone.
  • > In case of ball halts(more than 10 seconds) which does not involve mistake of any participant, the game will restart from the nearest ‘PointStart’ marked on the arena as shown in the arena map.
  • > Also, both bots will be placed equal distance apart from the 'PointStart.
